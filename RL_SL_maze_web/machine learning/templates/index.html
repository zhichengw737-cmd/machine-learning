🧠 How Does the Ball's Brain Actually Work?

In AI, we use 4 secret rules to help the ball learn. Here is what they mean in our maze game:

🤖 What is Reinforcement Learning (RL)?

Imagine you are playing a brand-new video game, but the screen is completely black, and nobody told you the rules!

The only way to win is to walk around, press buttons, and see what happens. When you hear a "Ding!" and get points/rewards, you think, "Hey, I should do that again!".
When you hear a "Buzz!" and lose points, you think, "Oops, better avoid that!".

That is exactly what Reinforcement Learning is!
It is a type of AI learning where we don't give the ball a map or tell it what to do. Instead, the ball learns completely by trial and error—getting rewards for good moves and penalties for bad ones until it discovers the perfect path.

🎛️ The 4 Secret Tuning Knobs (Parameters):

🎮 1. Episodes

The "Video Game Rounds"

Think of an Episode as 1 challenge of a game. It starts the moment the ball is at the beginning, and ends when it either gets completely lost or successfully reaches the goal!

Every time you click "Train & Run", you are starting a brand new episode to see if the ball can do better this round.

In this maze game, there are 5 Episodes to train the ball. With each episode, it learns a little more about what the arrows mean and how to get closer to the goal!

🧪 2. Alpha (α)

The "Memory Power"

Alpha is the learning rate
It decides How fast the ball learn new steps.

High Alpha:
The ball only cares about its last step and forgets what it learned before.

Low Alpha:
The ball learns slowly, care more about its previous steps but need more time to learn the last step.

What is the optimal value?
Every model is different! Need to test so many times to find it.

In normal, the range is 10-5 to 10-3.

👓 3. Gamma (γ)

The "Future-Vision Telescope"

Gamma controls how much the ball values future rewards.

Low Gamma:
The ball only care about finding a quick +10 points (reward) right in front of its face.

High Gamma:
The ball wear magic telescope that let it look far down the path to hunt for the massive +100 point (reward) at the very end.
High Gamma helps the AI plan ahead!

🧭 4. Epsilon (ε)

The "Curiosity Meter"

Epsilon decides how much the ball will explore new actions instead of using known ones.

High Epsilon:
The ball acts like a wild explorer, ignoring what it already knows to try crazy new directions and look for secrets.

Low Epsilon:
The ball says "I'm playing it safe!" and strictly follows the path it already trusts to finish the map safely.
