📘 What is Supervised Learning?

Supervised learning means the AI learns from labeled examples provided by a teacher.

In this playground, the teacher shows the AI an image and provides the correct label, such as “This is a CAT” or “This is a DOG”. This process is called training.

At the same time, the teacher also highlights important features (such as ear shape or whiskers for cats, and snout shape for dogs). The AI does not discover these features by itself — instead, it learns and stores them from the teacher.

As the AI sees more examples, it strengthens its understanding of these features. This is why more training leads to higher confidence in predictions.

When given a new image, the AI compares what it sees with what it has learned, estimates probabilities for each class, and chooses the most likely one. If the features are mixed (like in the final challenge), the AI may still make a prediction, but with lower confidence.

In this playground, the AI is shown images together with their correct labels: “This is a CAT” or “This is a DOG”. This process is called training.

After seeing enough different examples, the AI extracts key features (like ear shape or whisker patterns) to distinguish between classes. Once it gathers enough mathematical feature maps, it can recognize new images it has never seen before. This is the goal of supervised learning.
